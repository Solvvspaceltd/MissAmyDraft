Daily Routine

A day at Miss Amy's Daycare

Every day is carefully structured to balance learning, play, rest and nourishment — giving children security, stimulation and joy.

7:30am
Settling in

Morning welcome & free play

Children arrive, settle into the environment and choose from a range of open-ended play activities, construction toys, books and creative resources.

8:30am
Nutrition

Breakfast

A healthy, balanced breakfast together — encouraging independence, conversation and positive social habits around mealtimes.

9:00am
EYFS Learning

Circle time & group learning

Songs, stories, phonics, early maths and topic-based learning in a group setting. Building language, listening and communication skills.

10:00am
Creative

Adult-led activity

A focused creative, scientific or sensory activity linked to the weekly theme — arts, crafts, experiments, or cooking activities.

10:30am
Physical

Snack & outdoor play

Healthy mid-morning snack followed by outdoor garden time — gross motor play, nature exploration and fresh air.

12:00pm
Nutrition

Lunchtime

A nutritious, balanced lunch. Children are encouraged to develop independence, table manners and positive relationships around food.

1:00pm
Rest & recovery

Rest time / quiet activities

Sleep or rest for younger children. Quiet reading, puzzles, drawing or calm creative activities for older children.

2:30pm
Child-led

Child-initiated play

Free-flow child-led play across all areas of the setting — building on individual interests, peer relationships and imagination.

3:00pm
Creative

Afternoon activity & snack

Themed creative or sensory activities, afternoon snack, and time for school-age children arriving for wraparound care.

5:00pm
Winding down

Tidy-up & calm activities

Tidying together, calm story time and preparing for home. A gentle end to the day that supports transitions.

5:30pm
Home time

Parent handover

End-of-day handover with parents and carers, including verbal updates on the day's highlights, wellbeing and any notes to share.

Our Approach

Learning principles behind every day

EYFS Framework

Every activity is mapped to the Early Years Foundation Stage, supporting all seven areas of learning and development.

Play-based learning

We believe children learn best through play. Every resource and routine is designed to spark curiosity and discovery.

Emotional wellbeing first

A child who feels safe and valued is a child who can learn. Emotional security underpins everything we do.
